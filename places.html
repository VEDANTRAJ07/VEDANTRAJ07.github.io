Siddheshwar Temple [cite: 14]

Siddheshwar Temple is one of the most important spiritual landmarks in Solapur. [cite: 15] Dedicated to Lord Siddheshwar, a form of Lord Shiva, the temple is located near the peaceful Siddheshwar Lake in the center of the city. [cite: 16] Thousands of devotees visit the temple every day. [cite: 17] The temple becomes especially crowded during the Siddheshwar Yatra festival held every year in January. [cite: 17]

Pandharpur [cite: 18]

Pandharpur is one of the most sacred pilgrimage towns in Maharashtra and is located about 70 km from Solapur. [cite: 19] It is famous for the Vitthal Rukmini Temple dedicated to Lord Vitthal. [cite: 20] The town becomes a major spiritual center during the Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi festivals when millions of devotees participate in the Wari pilgrimage. [cite: 21]

Akkalkot [cite: 22]

Akkalkot is another important spiritual destination located about 40 km from Solapur. [cite: 23] It is famous for the temple of Shri Swami Samarth Maharaj. [cite: 24] Devotees believe Swami Samarth blesses people and helps them overcome difficulties in life. [cite: 25] The temple atmosphere is calm and peaceful, attracting visitors from all over the country. [cite: 26]

Solapur Fort [cite: 27]

Solapur Fort, also called Bhuikot Fort, is a historic monument located in the city. [cite: 28] The fort was built during the Bahmani Sultanate period and later ruled by different dynasties. [cite: 29] The structure reflects the military architecture of medieval India and represents the rich historical heritage of Solapur. [cite: 30]
